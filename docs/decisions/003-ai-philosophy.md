# ADR-003: AI Philosophy — Researcher in Control

## Status
Accepted

## Date
2026-04-04

## Context
In 2026, AI writing tools are everywhere but researchers distrust them for good reasons:
- University policies against AI-generated text
- Risk of plagiarism accusations
- AI-written related work is generic and lacks critical thinking
- Academic integrity is non-negotiable

We need to decide how AI is used in our product.

## Options considered
1. **AI writes for the researcher** — generates full paragraphs, sections. Pros: impressive demo. Cons: researchers won't trust it, ethical issues, generic output.
2. **AI augments the researcher** — helps discover, organize, visualize, suggest connections. Never writes final text. Pros: trustworthy, aligns with academic ethics. Cons: less "wow" in demos.
3. **Hybrid** — AI suggests drafts but researcher must edit. Pros: saves time. Cons: grey area ethically.

## Decision
**Option 2: AI augments, never writes.** The researcher is always in control. AI powers discovery, mapping, clustering, and connection-finding. The human thinks, reads, and writes.

This is not just an ethical choice — it's a product positioning choice. In a market flooded with "AI writes your paper" tools, we stand for quality and integrity.

## Consequences
- All AI outputs are suggestions, visualizations, or structured data — never final text
- Marketing emphasizes "superpowers for researchers" not "AI does your research"
- Builds trust with academic community
- Long-term: positions us as the serious tool, not a shortcut

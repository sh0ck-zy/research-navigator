# Feedback plan — first session with Rabanadas

The corpus is **his field** (functional neuroimaging — fMRI, connectivity,
connectome). Goal of this round: learn whether The Observatory earns a place in
his real workflow, not whether he finds it pretty.

## Method
- Send him the Space URL. **Let him drive, alone, on his own field.** Watch
  (screenshare), don't pitch. Stay quiet; note where he hesitates or lights up.
- Only after he's explored, ask the 5 questions below. Open questions — let him talk.

## The 5 questions
1. **Discovery** — Did the map show you anything you didn't know was there, or a
   connection between areas you hadn't considered?
2. **Search** — When you described a concept, did it surface relevant papers?
   Better or worse than how you find papers today?
3. **Workflow fit** — Would you actually save papers here and export BibTeX, or do
   you live in Zotero? What would have to be true for you to switch?
4. **Retention** — What's missing for you to open this on a random Tuesday?
   *(the question that matters most)*
5. **The honest test** — Does it feel like a tool you'd use, or a pretty demo?

## What a good signal looks like
- He saves papers without being told to, and asks "can it do X for my workflow".
- He names a specific recurring task it would replace or speed up.
- Bad signal: "cool" + no specific use. Then the wedge isn't there yet.
